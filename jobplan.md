## Market Fundamentals

### The Core Dynamic
There is a **massive shortage** of people who can:
- Build production AI systems using modern orchestration tools
- Ship working infrastructure using AI-assisted development (Claude Code, Cursor, etc.)
- Understand both business context and technical implementation
- Move fast with AI tools rather than traditional engineering practices

This shortage creates **pricing anomalies** where traditional leveling (IC3-IC7) breaks down. Companies in "panic mode" will pay $500K-$900K for someone who's already built what they're trying to figure out.

### What Makes This Market Different
1. **Recency matters more than tenure**: Someone who built production MCP systems in May 2025 is more valuable than a 10-year engineer who hasn't touched MCP
2. **Proof of work > credentials**: Shipping production systems beats CS degrees
3. **AI-assisted development is a feature, not a bug**: Companies increasingly value speed over "pure" coding ability
4. **Roles are still being defined**: Job titles don't map cleanly to traditional categories

### The Talent War Context
- Meta offered $100M+ packages to top AI talent (first year of multi-year deals)
- Meta's highest-paid research engineers: $440K base (before stock/bonus)
- OpenAI/Anthropic competing aggressively for anyone who can ship AI products
- Monthly job postings for Forward Deployed Engineers grew **800% Jan-Sep 2025**
- Anthropic posted their first "MCP Engineer" role in September 2025 (the protocol launched April 2024)

## Compensation Framework

### Base Salary Bands (Cash Component)
- **Junior roles (0-3 years relevant)**: $120K-$180K
- **Mid-level (3-5 years relevant)**: $150K-$250K
- **Senior (5-8 years relevant)**: $200K-$300K
- **Staff/Principal (8+ years relevant)**: $250K-$400K
- **Leadership/Executive**: $300K-$500K+

**Important**: In AI talent war, "relevant experience" is warped. 18 months of production MCP work can count as "5 years relevant" because almost nobody has done it.

### Total Compensation (Base + Bonus + Equity)
**Startups (Series A-B)**:
- Lower cash ($150K-$250K)
- Higher equity (0.5-2% for early, 0.1-0.5% for later)
- Equity could be worth $0 or $5M-$50M depending on outcome
- 4-year vest with 1-year cliff typically

**Startups (Series C-D)**:
- Medium cash ($200K-$300K)
- Medium equity (0.1-0.3%)
- More likely to have liquidity event (exit/IPO)
- Less upside than earlier stage

**Public Companies (Meta, Google, etc.)**:
- High cash ($200K-$400K)
- RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) - real value, vests over 4 years
- Less upside but guaranteed (stock is liquid)
- Meta/Google typically 50-60% of comp is stock

**Model Companies (Anthropic, OpenAI)**:
- High cash ($250K-$400K+)
- Equity varies (stock options, could be illiquid for years)
- Massive upside potential if company goes public/exits
- Risk: illiquidity, uncertain timeline

### The $350K+ Floor (For Target Profile)
To hit $350K+ total comp with equity, you need:

**Path 1: High base + moderate equity**
- $280K base + 0.15% at Series C ($500M valuation) = ~$350K year 1

**Path 2: Medium base + high equity**
- $220K base + 0.4% at Series B ($300M valuation) = ~$340K year 1

**Path 3: Public company RSUs**
- $200K base + $150K RSUs = $350K year 1

**Path 4: Model company all-in**
- $300K base + options/equity that may be worth millions (or zero)

**Path 5: Talent war package**
- Companies in panic mode: $350K-$500K cash + equity to get someone NOW

### Equity Considerations
**Dilution**: Your percentage will get diluted in future rounds. 0.2% at Series B might be 0.1% by IPO.

**Liquidity timeline**: 
- Public company RSUs: Sell immediately when vested
- Late-stage startup: Maybe 2-3 years to exit
- Early-stage: 5-10 years to exit (or never)

**Tax treatment**:
- RSUs: Taxed as income when vested
- ISOs: Potentially better tax treatment but complex
- NSOs: Taxed as income when exercised

**The 10x scenario**: 
- Join Series B at $500M valuation with 0.2% = $1M paper value
- Company goes to $5B (10x) = $10M paper value
- After dilution (50%) = $5M actual value
- After taxes = $2.5M-3M in pocket

This is why equity matters more than small base differences.

## The MCP Factor: Why It Changes Everything

### What MCP Actually Is
Model Context Protocol (launched April 2024 by Anthropic) is a standardized way for AI models to connect to external data sources and tools. Think of it as "how AI connects to the world."

### Why MCP Experience is Valuable
1. **Recency**: Protocol is <18 months old
2. **Scarcity**: Very few people have built production MCP systems
3. **Relevance**: Every AI company needs MCP integration
4. **Proof of concept**: Having production MCP = you actually shipped, not just read docs

### MCP Market Timeline
- **April 2024**: MCP protocol launched by Anthropic
- **May 2025**: Early adopters building first production systems (<<1000 people globally)
- **September 2025**: Anthropic posts first "MCP Engineer" job posting
- **November 2025**: Still <5000 people globally with production MCP experience (estimated)
- **2026 projection**: MCP becomes standard, advantage window closes

### The Valuation Multiplier
Traditional engineering: "I have 5 years of Python experience" = commodity skill

MCP in November 2025: "I built production MCP systems in May 2025" = scarce skill

This scarcity justifies **2-3x normal comp** for roles where MCP matters (Solutions Architect at Anthropic, Applied AI at OpenAI, etc.)

### Where MCP Experience Matters Most
**Critical (3x value multiplier)**:
- Anthropic (they created it)
- AI integration companies
- Developer tools building on MCP

**Important (2x value multiplier)**:
- OpenAI (competing protocol)
- Other model companies
- AI application companies

**Nice to have (1.5x multiplier)**:
- Enterprise AI adoption
- Consulting firms
- Big Tech AI teams

### MCP as Positioning Tool
Instead of: "I'm a developer who uses AI tools"
Say: "I built production MCP infrastructure when <1000 people globally had done it"

This frames you as **early adopter in critical technology**, not "person trying to break into AI."

## The AI-Assisted Development Context

### Why This Matters
The market is split on AI-assisted development:
- **Old guard**: "Real engineers write code from scratch"
- **New guard**: "Fast shipping matters more than code purity"

You need to find companies in the "new guard" camp.

### How to Frame It
**Don't say**: "I don't really code, AI does it for me"
**Do say**: "I use AI-assisted development (Claude Code, Cursor) to ship production systems 10x faster than traditional methods"

**Don't say**: "I can't debug without AI"

### Companies That Get It

- Anthropic (obviously)

This frames AI-assisted development as a **strategic advantage**, not a limitation.

## Negotiation Frameworks

### The Competing Offers Strategy
1. **Never accept first offer**: Always negotiate
2. **Create competition**: "I'm in late stages with [Company X]" (even if just initial conversations)
3. **Let them outbid**: "I'm really excited about this role. Company X is offering $Y. Can you match?"
4. **Equity vs cash**: "I'd take lower base for more equity" or vice versa

### The Talent War Frame
"I know I don't fit the traditional mold, but I've built production systems that most engineers are still trying to understand. In the current AI talent market, that's worth a premium."

### The Remote Negotiation
**After getting offer**:
"I'd love to join. One thing - I'd like to work from [LATAM city]. I'll be in the same timezone, always available for meetings, and can fly in quarterly for team events. Does that work?"

If they say no, counter:
"What if we do a 6-month trial? If it's not working, I'll relocate."

### The Equity Maximization
If you believe in the company:
"I'd take $250K base instead of $300K if you can give me an extra 0.1% equity."

At a $500M valuation, 0.1% = $500K paper value. If company 10x's, you just turned $50K cash into $5M equity.

### The Floor Setting
"My floor is $350K total comp with meaningful equity. I'm talking to companies in the $400K-$500K range, but I'm most excited about this role. What can you do?"

This sets expectations without being aggressive.

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Company Red Flags
- **"We're looking for a full-stack engineer who can also do PM work"** = They want 2 roles for 1 salary
- **Vague about equity details** = Probably bad equity terms
- **"We move fast and break things"** = Usually means chaos, not speed
- **No clear product-market fit** = Equity will be worthless
- **Founder is "visionary" but can't articulate strategy** = Run

### Role Red Flags
- **Job description has 47 required skills** = They don't know what they need
- **"Wear many hats"** = You'll do everything, master nothing
- **Reports to multiple people** = Recipe for misery
- **No clear success metrics** = You'll never know if you're succeeding
- **"We need someone who can hit the ground running with zero support"** = They don't onboard or train

### Compensation Red Flags
- **All equity, low cash** = Only works if you have runway
- **"We'll give you a raise after proving yourself"** = You're underpaid now and will be underpaid later
- **Stock options with 10-year exercise window** = Probably worthless
- **No clear path to liquidity** = Your equity may never be worth anything
- **"Market rate"** without specifics = Below market rate

### Culture Red Flags
- **Leadership doesn't use the product** = They don't believe in it
- **High turnover** = Something is broken
- **All-male/all-white leadership** = Limited perspectives, potential bias issues
- **"We're a family"** = Toxic boundary violations incoming
- **Unlimited PTO** = Nobody takes PTO
